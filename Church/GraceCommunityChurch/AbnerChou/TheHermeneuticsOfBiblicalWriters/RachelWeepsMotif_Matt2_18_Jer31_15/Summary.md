
## Problem:
- Some suggest that Matt 2:18 is taking out of context of the original text.
	- Jeremiah is talking about Israel’s deportation and exile, not Herod’s killing of children in Bethlehem

## Solution
1. **Describe the entire era:** Jer 31:15 describes the entire era of exile. Must have begin with initial deportation but it continues.
	1. Participle denoting characteristic action (מְבַכָּ֣ה) .
		1. Actively and continuously weeping. Not like once wept
		2. Rachel is the wife of Jacob, state of a weeping as a mother
	2. **Rachel to stop weeping** when the new covenant comes. Jer 31:31 reaffirms.
	3. Other books use motif of Jeremiah 31: 15 to describe other parts of exile
		1. Lam 2: 12 - Motif of "mothers and the loss of children" is repeated descriptor of **exile** 
		2. Ramah is a place of repeated exilic events and shows grief
			1. Ramah probably north
			2. But not just reference for north but southern kingdom as well - 40:1
			3. Various stages - Hos 5: 8, Isa 10:29
2. Conclusion of Matt 2:18
	1. **As another case**: Matthew case is shown as another case of the pain of exile like Jer 31:15
	2. **Critical point**: Reaches a critical point of fulfillment due to arrival of the prophesied "Branch" who bring restoration and the new covenant.
		1. Isa 11:1, Jer 23: 5
		2. Nazarene can mean "branch"
	3. **Climax of tears**: Climatic and end of tears of Rachel with the tears of mothers of Bethlehem.
		1.  Exile is over
		2. Son of God arrived
		3. Will introduce the New Covenant later in Jer 31:31 
			- (bad citation in the book)
	4. **Motif of exilic suffering**: Matthew continues the exilic suffering
		1. originally from Jer 31:15
		2. maintained by the prophets
	5. **Predicting the end of exile**: Predictive elements in Jer 31:15 to end exile.

Conclusion
- i think the end is not over until Israel as a nation accepts the new covenant.
