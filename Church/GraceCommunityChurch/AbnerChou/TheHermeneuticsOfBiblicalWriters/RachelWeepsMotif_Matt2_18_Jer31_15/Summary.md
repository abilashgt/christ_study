
## Problem:
- Some suggest that Matt 2:18 is taking out of context of the original text.
	- 

## Solution
1. **Describe the entire era:** Jer 31:15 describes the entire era. Must have begin with initial deportation but it continues.
2. **Rachel to stop weeping** when the new covenant comes. Jer 31:31 reaffirms.
3. Other books (Prophets)
	1. Motif of "mothers and the loss of children" is repeated descriptor of **exile** - Lam 2: 12
	2. Ramah is a place of repeated exilic events and shows grief
		1. Ramah probably north
		2. But not just reference for north but southern kingdom as well - 40:1
		3. Various stages - Hos 5: 8, Isa 10:29

### Conclusion of Matt 2:18
1. **As another case**: Matthew is shown as another case of Jer 31:15
2. **Critical point**: Reaches a critical point of fulfillment due to arrival of the prophesied "Branch" who bring restoration and the new covenant.
	1. Isa 11:1, Jer 23: 5
	2. Nazarene mean "branch"
3. **Climax of tears**: Climatic and end of tears of Rachel with the tears of mothers of Bethlehem.
	1.  Exile is over
	2. Son of God arrived
	3. Introduction of New Covenant Jer 26:24
4. **Motif of exilic suffering**: Matthew continues the exilic suffering
	1. originally from Jer 31:15
	2. maintained by the prophets
5. **Predicting the end of exile**: Predictive elements in Jer 31:15 to end exile.
